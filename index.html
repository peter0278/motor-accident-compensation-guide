Understanding the Queensland CTP Scheme

The Compulsory Third Party (CTP) scheme is a mandatory insurance policy attached to the registration of every motor vehicle in Queensland.

What is CTP?

CTP insurance indemnifies vehicle owners and drivers who are legally liable for personal injury to another person. It covers personal injury only and does not cover vehicle or property damage.

Who is Covered?

  • Drivers (not fully at fault)
  • Passengers
  • Pedestrians
  • Cyclists
  • Motorcyclists
  • Pillion passengers

Immediate Steps After a Motor Vehicle Accident

1. At the Scene

Your priority is safety, but once the danger has passed, gather evidence including photos, witness details, and driver information.

2. Seek Medical Attention Immediately

Medical documentation creates the legal link between the accident and your injuries.

3. Reporting to Police

You must report the crash to police and obtain a Police Report Number before lodging a CTP claim.

Motorcycle Accident Claims: A Unique Category

Riders face significantly higher injury risks and claims often involve higher compensation due to the severity of injuries.

Understanding Motorcycle accident claims is critical due to common insurer bias against riders.

What Can You Be Compensated For?
